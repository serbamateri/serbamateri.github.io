2. Dayang Sumbi did not marry her dog, Tumang, in the folklore. The story goes that Dayang Sumbi, wanting to deter Sangkuriang from pursuing her, asked him to bring her a set of impossible tasks, including hunting and bringing her a deer's liver. However, Sangkuriang failed to complete the tasks, so Dayang Sumbi tricked him by using a dog's liver instead. There is no mention of Dayang Sumbi marrying her dog.

3. In the folklore, Sangkuriang killed his dog because he was frustrated and angry after failing to complete the tasks given by Dayang Sumbi. He didn't recognize that the liver he obtained was from his own dog, Tumang.

4. After realizing that Sangkuriang had killed Tumang, Dayang Sumbi was filled with grief and regret. She mourned the loss of her loyal companion and decided to leave the village, taking Tumang's body with her.

5. Dayang Sumbi avoided marrying Sangkuriang because she eventually discovered that Sangkuriang was her own son, whom she had given up for adoption years ago. Realizing the incestuous relationship, she wanted to prevent the marriage from happening and tried to set impossible tasks for Sangkuriang to discourage him.
