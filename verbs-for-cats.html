18. BERIKAN CONTOH SELAIN DIBAWAH INI Sentence Structure Pattern 1. Subject + Verb The verbs used in this pattern are intransitive verbs or verbs that don't have an object, for example: The bird flies The boy cried ✓ ✓


Jawaban:

Subject + Verb + Object

This is one of the most common sentence structure patterns. The subject

performs the action and the object receives the action.

For example:

She eats an apple.

They watch a movie.

He reads a book.

Subject + Verb + Indirect Object + Direct Object

In this pattern, the subject performs the action, the direct object receives the action, and the indirect object identifies to or for whom the action is done.

For example:

He gave his sister a gift.

She sent her friend a letter.

The teacher gave the students an assignment.

26. In writing a report text, we will find several sentences that are written using link verbs or relating verbs, what is the most appropriate reason for using linking/relating verbs?​


Jawaban:

klik bintang

Penjelasan:

Linking or relating verbs are used in sentences to establish a connection or relationship between the subject of the sentence and a subject complement, which can be a noun, pronoun, or adjective. These verbs don't show action but instead describe a state of being or a condition. The most appropriate reasons for using linking or relating verbs in a report text include:

Describing Characteristics or Traits: Linking verbs are often used to describe the characteristics, qualities, or traits of the subject. They help provide a clear understanding of what the subject is like. For example: "The climate in the region is humid."

Providing Information: Linking verbs help provide essential information about the subject, helping readers understand the subject's state or condition. For instance: "The experiment was successful."

Explaining Relationships: Linking verbs can be used to explain the relationships between different components in the subject matter. For example: "The economy is closely tied to technological advancements."

Expressing States of Being: Linking verbs are used to express a state of being or existence. They can highlight a subject's current condition, state, or situation. For instance: "The patient seems weak."

Reporting Facts: Linking verbs are used to report facts or truths. They help convey information that is generally accepted or widely known. For example: "The Earth is a planet."

Describing Changes: Linking verbs can describe changes or developments in a subject's condition or state over time. For example: "The city has become more populous."

Comparing and Contrasting: Linking verbs are useful when comparing or contrasting different subjects based on their attributes. For instance: "The new design is more efficient than the previous one."

Highlighting Importance or Significance: Linking verbs can emphasize the importance or significance of a subject or idea. For example: "The discovery is significant for medical research."

Providing Objective Information: Linking verbs are used to provide objective information that doesn't involve personal interpretation or bias. They contribute to a neutral tone in reporting. For instance: "The data appear consistent."

Overall, linking or relating verbs help establish a clear and direct connection between the subject and the description or attribute being conveyed. They play a crucial role in presenting information in a report text by describing, defining, and explaining various aspects of the subject matter.
