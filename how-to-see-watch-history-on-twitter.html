The theory of literature investigates the general laws of the structure and development of literature. The history of literature studies the literary past as a process or one of the stages of this process. Literary criticism is concerned with the most recent, the “present” state of literature.

History of Basket Weaving

Basket weaving dates back a very long time. In fact it pre-dates some forms of pottery and woven cloth. Evidence for this has been discovered in the form of stone carvings from around 20,000 years BC. The materials used would have depended on people’s surroundings and varied considerably, from willow to roots, brambles, vines, oak, ash, hazel, bamboo, leaves, straw, rush and bark. Some things were woven, others were coiled.

Baskets were woven and lined with clay to create waterproof bowls. Houses were built with basket work (wattle) and daubed with mud, right up until the 1700s. Men would go to battle with wickerwork shields, babies were put in woven cribs, and people were buried in woven coffins. Gypsies would make baskets from hedgerow materials, as did many more people during the Second World War, because all the willow (osier) beds were reserved for baskets to transport food and ammunition for the troops.

Two hundred years ago, willow was a huge industry. Weavers lived around areas that grew willow, and this included East Anglia. Basketry was a hugely popular form of packaging – it was used for transporting fish, dairy products, brick, stone, coal, and manure and also for woven animal muzzles, bird traps, eel traps, lobster pots, and coracles. Each area within a country usually had its own styles of baskets and containers, which could be very different.

36. how do museums take part in the history of human civilization​


Jawaban:

Museums play a crucial role in the history of human civilization by preserving and showcasing artifacts and objects that are significant to different cultures, societies, and historical periods. They collect and display works of art, sculptures, manuscripts, artifacts, and other materials that reflect the diverse experiences and achievements of humanity. These collections provide a window into the past and serve as a valuable resource for researchers, educators, and the general public. Museums also provide educational programs, guided tours, and other interactive experiences that help visitors gain a deeper understanding of history and culture. Additionally, many museums work to increase access to their collections and resources through digital platforms, making them widely available to people all over the world. Overall, museums play a vital role in preserving and sharing the history of human civilization and they are an essential part of our cultural heritage
