4. If the focus of convex lens is 20 cm, the power of lens is

Before we calculate about the power of any lens, we must know first the type of lens. There are 2 type of lens named convex and concave. The relationship between the focus of lens and the power of the lens dan be calculate with this formula :

If the type of lens is concave lens

[tex] \boxed{P = - \frac{100}{f}}[/tex]

If the type of lens is convex lens

[tex] \boxed{P = \frac{100}{f}}[/tex]

If :

P = lens power (D) f = lens focus (cm) Work Steps :

We know that :

f = lens focus = 20 cm Type of lens = convex

Question : P = lens power = ... cm

Answer :

[tex] P = \frac{100}{f}[/tex]

[tex] P = \frac{100}{20} = \boxed {5 \: D} [/tex]

15. A solution of H2SO4 with a molal concentration of 8.010 m has adensity of 1.354g/mL. What is the molar concentration of thissolution?​


Answer with explanation:

You are given a solution of sulphuric acid with a molal concentration of 8.010 m and its density is 1.354 g/mL.

And we also know that the molar mass of sulphuric acid which is 98.079 g/mol.

◈ ◈ ◈

With the known datas, we work out the molar concentration in mol/L.

◈ ◈ ◈

The relation between molarity, molality and density is described within the following equation.

[tex]\frac{1}{m} = \frac{d}{M}-\frac{Mr}{1000}[/tex]

Where,

Density (d) = 1.354 g/mL

Molecular mass of solute (Mr) = 98.079 g/mol

Molality (m) = 8.010 m

Molarity (M) = ... mol/L

◈ ◈ ◈

So then,

[tex]\frac{1}{8.010\ m} = \frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M}-\frac{98.079\ g/mol}{1000}[/tex]

[tex]0.1248\ m = \frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M}-0.098079\ g/mol[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M} = 0.098079\ g/mol+ 0.1248\ m[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1.354\ g/mL}{M} = 0.222879\ g/mmol[/tex]

[tex]M = \frac{1.354\ g/mL}{0.222879\ g/mmol}[/tex]

[tex]M = 6.075\ mol/L[/tex]

◈ ◈ ◈

Therefore, the molar concentration of said acid solution is 6.075 mol/L.

◈ ◈ ◈

37. 12. Mixing 100 ml of 0.1 M KCI aqueous solution with 100 ml of 0.1 M NaCl aqueous solution, what are the molar concentrations of K+, Na+ and Cl- ions in the final solution?​


Jawaban:

To find the molar concentrations of K+, Na+, and Cl- ions in the final solution, we need to consider the principles of stoichiometry and the fact that KCl and NaCl dissociate in water.

First, let's calculate the moles of KCl and NaCl in the initial solutions:

Moles of KCl = Volume (L) x Molarity (mol/L) = 0.1 L x 0.1 mol/L = 0.01 mol
