8. explain the formation of shadows in the human eye! Pls hurry (English Pls)


Jawaban:

The process of creating a shadow in the eye is this: Impulses come in waves of light entering the eye through the cornea will pass through the pupil whose pupil is fixed by the iris (light intensity regulated by the iris), then light will be refracted by the eye's lens, creating a viscous, inverted, subtracted shadow and then it will be forwarded to the rods and cones which will continue the light signals through the optic nerve to translate the impulses, The image will form on its object.

the cornea serves as a window and as an entrance to light into the eye for clear words and images.The anterior eye chamber is a jelly-like sac located behind the cornea of the eye, in front of the lensaidal humor which helps bring nutrients to the eye's tissues serves as a pressure counterweight inside the eye.The sclera is a hard white membrane with fibrosa tissue that covers the entire eyeball except the cornea.The iris and the pupil are part of the anatomy of the eyes that relate to each other. The iris is a ring-shaped membrane inside the eye that surrounds the hole in the middle. The hole in the middle is what the pupil is called. The pupil isa muscle that closes and opens or grows smaller and larger.The lens is a translucent, flexible network located right behind the iris and the pupil. The function of the lens is to help focus light and images on the retina.Choroid and conjunctiva are dark brown membranes with large blood vessels inside. Its position is between the sklera and the retina. This choroid serves to supply blood and nutrients to the retina and to all other structures in the anatomy of the eye. The conjunctivitis, on the other hand, are the transparent layers that coat the cornea and the eyelid.Vitreous humor is a jelly-like substance that fills the inside of the back of the eye.The retina and the optic are a light-sensitive network. 25. 13. Ahmad is observing the islamic boarding school's mosque along with the minaret that nearby. The mosque and its minaret are 45m and 60m hig respectively. At that time the su elevation angle formed was 60°. The difference between the shadows of the minaret and mosque is​


Jawaban:

We can start by drawing a diagram of the situation:

```

|

|\

| \

| \ 60°

| \

|____\

60m

---------------

45m

```

From the diagram, we can see that we have a right triangle with the minaret height (60m) as the opposite side and the distance between Ahmad and the base of the minaret (which we'll call x) as the adjacent side. We can use trigonometry to find x:

```

tan(60°) = opposite / adjacent

tan(60°) = 60 / x

x = 60 / tan(60°)

x = 34.64 (rounded to two decimal places)

```

Now we can find the length of the shadow of the minaret. We know that the angle of elevation of the sun is complementary to the angle of elevation of the minaret (since the sun's rays are parallel), so we can find the angle of elevation of the sun:

angle of elevation of sun = 90° - 60°

angle of elevation of sun = 30°

We also know that the length of the shadow of the minaret is directly proportional to the height of the minaret. Therefore:

length of shadow of minaret / minaret height = x / (x + 45)

Solving for the length of the shadow, we get:

length of shadow of minaret = minaret height * (x / (x + 45))

length of shadow of minaret = 60 * (34.64 / (34.64 + 45))

length of shadow of minaret = 32.14 (rounded to two decimal places)

Similarly, we can find the length of the shadow of the mosque. This time, the height we're using is 45m, and the distance from Ahmad to the base of the mosque is x + 45:

length of shadow of mosque / mosque height = (x + 45) / x

Solving for the length of the shadow, we get:

length of shadow of mosque = mosque height * ((x + 45) / x)

length of shadow of mosque = 45 * ((34.64 + 45) / 34.64)

length of shadow of mosque = 52.71 (rounded to two decimal places)

Finally, we can find the difference between the shadows of the minaret and mosque:

difference = length of shadow of mosque - length of shadow of minaret

difference = 52.71 - 32.14

difference = 20.57 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, the difference between the shadows of the minaret and mosque is 20.57 meters.
