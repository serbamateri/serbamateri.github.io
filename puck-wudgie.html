Identify the subjects and verbs in the following sentences by writing an "s" above the subject and a "v" above the verb. identify the types of clauses by underlining independent clauses once and dependent clauses twice. then indicate which type of sentence each one is. 1. with lightning speed, the defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot blasted the puck over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net. 2. to sam, nothing in the world could compare to the delicious gingerbread that his grandmother prepared for christmas each year.


Jawaban:

1. **With lightning speed**, the defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot **blasted** the puck over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net.

- Independent Clause (IC): The defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot blasted the puck over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net.

- Type of Sentence: Complex

2. **To Sam**, nothing in the world could compare to the delicious gingerbread that his grandmother prepared for Christmas each year.

- Independent Clause (IC): Nothing in the world could compare to the delicious gingerbread that his grandmother prepared for Christmas each year.

- Dependent Clause (DC): To Sam

- Type of Sentence: Complex

Subject (s) and Verb (v) breakdown:

1. **With lightning speed**, (no subject)

- the defenseman’s slapshot **blasted** (s, v)

- the puck (s)

- over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net (no verb)

2. **To Sam**, (no verb)

- nothing (s)

- in the world could compare (no subject, v)

- to the delicious gingerbread that his grandmother prepared for Christmas each year (no subject, v)

Identify the subjects and verbs in the following sentences by writing an "s" above the subject and a "v" above the verb. identify the types of clauses by underlining independent clauses once and dependent clauses twice. then indicate which type of sentence each one is. 1. with lightning speed, the defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot blasted the puck over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net. 2. to sam, nothing in the world could compare to the delicious gingerbread that his grandmother prepared for christmas each year.


Penjelasan:

1. With lightning speed, the defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot blasted the puck over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net.

\(s\) defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot

\(v\) blasted

Independent Clause (underlined once):

"the defenseman’s perfectly-placed slapshot blasted the puck over the goalie’s shoulder and into the top left corner of the net"

This is a **simple sentence** as it has just one independent clause.

2. To Sam, nothing in the world could compare to the delicious gingerbread that his grandmother prepared for Christmas each year.

\(s\) nothing

\(v\) could compare

\(s\) his grandmother

\(v\) prepared

Independent Clause (underlined once):

"nothing in the world could compare to the delicious gingerbread"

Dependent Clause (underlined twice):

"that his grandmother prepared for Christmas each year"

This is a **complex sentence** as it has one independent clause and at least one dependent clause.
